How to Download Your Instagram Data (2026 Step-by-Step Guide)

Unfollow Finder Team 5 min read Updated July 2026

Instagram lets you download a complete copy of your own data — your followers, the accounts you follow, your posts, messages, and more. It's free, it's built into the app, and it's the safest way to access your own information.

This guide walks you through exactly how to download your Instagram data in 2026, step by step.

Why download your Instagram data?

There are a few good reasons to grab a copy of your data:

  • See who unfollowed you — your followers and following lists are included, so you can compare them
  • Keep a backup — save your posts, photos, and messages
  • Check who doesn't follow you back — compare your two lists
  • Privacy — access your data without giving any third-party app your password

Which format should you choose: JSON or HTML?

When you request your data, Instagram asks whether you want it in JSON or HTML format.

  • JSON — best if you want to analyze your data with a tool (like checking unfollowers). Choose this one.
  • HTML — best if you just want to browse your data in a web page.
Tip

If your goal is to see who unfollowed you or who doesn't follow you back, always choose JSON. Tools read this format directly.

Step-by-step: Download your Instagram data

Step 1 — Open your account settings

Open the Instagram app and go to your profile. Tap the menu (three lines) in the top-right corner, then tap Settings and privacy.

Step 2 — Open Accounts Centre

Near the top, tap Accounts Centre (it may say "Meta Accounts Centre"). Then tap Your information and permissions.

Step 3 — Download your information

Tap Download your information, then tap Download or transfer information.

Step 4 — Choose your account

Select the Instagram account you want to download data for, then tap Next.

Step 5 — Pick your settings

Choose Download to device. Then set:

  • Date range: All time
  • Format: JSON
  • Media quality: whatever you prefer

Step 6 — Submit the request

Tap Create expots (or "Submit request"). Instagram will now prepare your data.

Step 7 — Wait for the email

Instagram will email you a download link when your file is ready. This usually takes a few minutes, but can take up to 48 hours depending on how much data you requested.

Step 9 — Download and unzip

Open the email, tap Download, and log in if asked. You'll get a ZIP file. Unzip it to see your data folders.

Where are your followers and following files?

After unzipping, look inside the connections folder, then followers_and_following. You'll find:

  • followers_1.json — everyone who follows you
  • following.json — everyone you follow

Note: if you have a lot of followers, there may be multiple files (followers_1.json, followers_2.json, and so on).

What can you do with your data?

Once you have your followers_1.json and following.json files, you can instantly find out:

  • Who unfollowed you
  • Who doesn't follow you back
  • Who your mutual followers are
