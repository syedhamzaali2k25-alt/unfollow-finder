How to Hide Your Followers List on Instagram

Unfollow Finder Team 6 min read Updated July 2026

By default, anyone who can see your Instagram profile can also see your full followers list — who follows you, and in what order. If you'd rather keep that list private, whether for general privacy, to avoid unwanted attention, or just because it feels like nobody else's business, there are a few real ways to limit who sees it. Here's what actually works.

Can you fully hide your followers list on Instagram?

This is worth being upfront about: Instagram doesn't offer a single dedicated setting that hides your followers list while keeping your account fully public. If your account is public, anyone can view your followers list by visiting your profile — there's no toggle that keeps the account visible but the list hidden. The real options are about who can see it, not making it invisible while staying fully open.

Option 1: Switch your account to private

This is the most complete option. When your account is private:

  1. Go to your profile and tap the menu icon
  2. Tap Settings and privacy
  3. Tap Account privacy
  4. Toggle Private account on

With a private account, only your approved followers can see your followers list, following list, posts, and Stories. Anyone else has to send a follow request first. This is the closest thing to genuinely hiding your followers list from the general public — but the tradeoff is that it also restricts everything else about your account, not just this one list.

Option 2: Limit who can see you through Close Friends and restricted interactions

If going fully private feels like too much, Instagram doesn't give you a middle-ground setting specifically for the followers list, but you can reduce who's paying attention to your account through related tools:

  • Restrict specific accounts (Settings → Privacy → Restricted Accounts) — this limits certain people's ability to see your activity without blocking them outright
  • Remove or block followers you don't want having visibility — this doesn't hide the list from the public, but it does control who's actually on it

Neither of these hides your list from everyone the way switching to private does — they're more about controlling specific relationships than achieving broad privacy.

Option 3: Business or creator account considerations

If you run a business or creator account, be aware that these account types are generally expected to stay public for discoverability and analytics purposes — switching to private limits some business features (like ads, insights on non-followers, and profile visits from search). If your goal is follower-list privacy but you rely on business features, going private involves a real tradeoff, and there isn't a way around it — Instagram doesn't offer a private-but-still-businesslike middle option for this specific concern.

What doesn't actually work

Some tools and browser extensions claim to let you view or hide follower lists in ways Instagram doesn't officially support — these typically require risky account access or don't function as advertised, since Instagram controls what's visible through the app and website directly rather than through a setting a third party can toggle for you. Be cautious of anything asking for your Instagram login to "hide" your followers — that's not how this actually works, and it introduces the same account risk as login-based unfollow trackers.

A related question: can you hide just your following list, not followers?

Yes — but the mechanism is the same. There's no separate setting for hiding only your following list while keeping followers visible, or vice versa. Going private affects both lists together, since Instagram treats them as one visibility setting, not two independent ones.
