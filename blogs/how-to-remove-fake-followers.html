How to Remove Fake Followers on Instagram (2026 Guide)

Unfollow Finder Team 6 min read Updated July 2026

Fake followers might make your follower count look bigger, but they quietly hurt your account in ways that matter. They drag down your engagement rate, distort your analytics, and can even make brands hesitant to work with you. The good news: finding and removing them is straightforward once you know what to look for.

This guide explains what fake followers are, why removing them helps, and exactly how to clean up your Instagram account step by step.

What are fake followers?

Fake followers are accounts that follow you but bring no real value. They generally fall into a few categories:

  • Bots — automated accounts with no real person behind them
  • Ghost followers — inactive accounts that never like, comment, or view your content
  • Spam accounts — accounts that exist only to post spam or scam links
  • Bought followers — fake accounts added through "buy followers" services

Why should you remove fake followers?

It might feel counterintuitive to reduce your follower count on purpose, but here's why it's worth it:

  • Higher engagement rate — engagement is measured against your follower count. Fewer fake followers means a healthier ratio.
  • Better reach — Instagram's algorithm favors accounts with genuine engagement, so cleaning up can actually help your posts reach more people.
  • Accurate analytics — real insights help you understand your true audience.
  • Brand trust — brands check engagement rates before partnering. Fake followers are a red flag.
  • Account safety — spam and bot accounts can be associated with suspicious activity.
Key point

A smaller, genuine following almost always performs better than a large following padded with bots.

How to spot fake followers

Before removing anyone, learn to recognize the signs of a fake account:

  • No profile picture, or a generic stock photo
  • No posts, or posts full of spam and links
  • A username with random letters and numbers
  • Following thousands of accounts but having very few followers
  • Never likes or comments on your content
  • Generic or repetitive spam comments

How to remove fake followers step by step

Step 1 — Identify who doesn't engage

Start by figuring out which followers never interact with your posts. These "ghost" followers are the biggest group to clean up. You can review your followers manually, or use a tool to see who follows you but never engages.

Step 2 — Review your followers list

Go to your profile and tap your followers count to open the list. Look for the fake-account signs above — no photo, spammy usernames, zero posts.

Step 3 — Remove followers individually

Instagram lets you remove a follower without blocking them. Open your followers list, tap the three dots next to a follower (or tap "Remove"), and confirm. They won't be notified that you removed them.

Step 4 — Block persistent spam or bot accounts

For aggressive spam or bot accounts that keep coming back, blocking is more effective than removing. Go to their profile, tap the menu, and select Block.

Step 5 — Report obvious spam

If an account is clearly spam or a bot, report it to Instagram. This helps keep the platform cleaner for everyone and can speed up removal.

Step 6 — Clean up regularly

Fake followers accumulate over time. Make it a habit to review your followers every month or two so your account stays healthy.

Should you use apps that auto-remove followers?

Be cautious. Apps that promise to automatically remove fake followers usually require full access to your account — including your password — and can violate Instagram's Terms of Service. This puts your account at risk of restrictions or suspension.

The safest approach is to identify fake followers using a privacy-friendly tool that reads your data export, then remove them manually through Instagram itself. It takes a little longer but keeps your account completely safe.
