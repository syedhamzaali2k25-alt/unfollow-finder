How to See Recent Followers and Following on Instagram

Unfollow Finder Team 6 min read Updated July 2026

Instagram's default followers and following lists don't make it obvious who's new. There's no big banner saying "these three people followed you this week" — you have to know where to look. Here's every actual way to see recent follow activity, both people who recently followed you and accounts you recently followed.

Seeing recent followers (people who just followed you)

Method 1: Your Activity tab

The most direct way to see new followers is the Activity tab (the heart icon). Instagram lists "started following you" notifications here in reverse chronological order — most recent at the top. This is the closest thing Instagram has to a dedicated "new followers" feed, and it updates in real time as people follow you.

The limitation: this tab shows a rolling window of recent activity, not a permanent, searchable log going back indefinitely. If you want to know who followed you last month, scrolling back that far in Activity isn't reliable.

Method 2: Sorting your followers list by date

Open your followers list from your profile, and check for a sort or filter option (usually a small icon near the search bar within the list). On many versions of the app, this lets you sort by "Date followed: Latest first" instead of the default order. This gives you a chronological view of your followers list itself, rather than just a temporary notification feed.

If you don't see a sort option on your version of the app, this feature has rolled out inconsistently across regions and app versions — in that case, the Activity tab or the data export method below are your reliable fallbacks.

Seeing recent following (accounts you recently followed)

This one is trickier, since Instagram doesn't surface "accounts you recently followed" as prominently as new-follower notifications.

Method 1: Sorting your following list

Same as above — open your following list and check for a sort option to view by most recently followed. Where available, this is the fastest way to see your own recent follow activity.

Method 2: Your account activity log

Instagram keeps a record of your own actions, including follows, under Settings → Your Activity → Recently deleted, likes, and other activity logs. Depending on your app version, following/unfollowing history may appear here as part of your broader activity record, though this section is more focused on content interactions than a clean following timeline.

Getting a permanent, reliable record

Both in-app methods above have a real limitation: they show recent activity within a rolling window, and the sort features aren't consistently available on every account. If you want a dependable, exportable record of your followers and following — including timestamps for when each relationship started — Instagram's own data export is the most reliable source.

Requesting your data through Settings → Your Activity → Download Your Information (JSON format) gives you your complete followers and following lists, each with a timestamp showing when the relationship began. Unlike the in-app views, this is a complete snapshot you can save, compare against a later export, and use to track changes over time — rather than relying on a notification feed that only shows a limited recent window. Why "recent" isn't the same as "new since last time you checked"

One distinction worth being clear on: Instagram's recent-activity views show recency in general — who followed you lately, in absolute terms. They don't specifically compare against a personal "last time I checked" baseline, the way a data-export comparison does. If you check your Activity tab every day, "recent" naturally aligns with "since I last looked." If you check less often, you may see a backlog of notifications rather than a clean list of exactly what changed since your last visit — which is where comparing two data exports becomes more precise than relying on the notification feed alone.
